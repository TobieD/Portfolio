My first introduction into Unreal Engine 4 and a modular workflow for creating 3D assets. The map was created using the shootergame project from the marketplace as a gameplay basis and build over a period of 3 months.

Playing with blocks

I started with blocking out some levels using the BSP tools of Unreal Engine 4, to have an idea how the map would play and if it was fun to play. I found that my first iterations were all very horizontal in design and played in a linear way, which isn`t something you want in a multiplayer map. After a couple of iterations I ended up with a map that had a mix of open floors, narrow paths and height variations.

When I had my block out, I needed to come up with a setting the map would be in. I decided to go for a factory setting and loosely basing myself on concept art of Deus Ex: Human Revolution, once I decided the setting I wanted to create the modular textures, a floor texture, a wall texture and a trim texture.

The modular approach

After this step I could finally start modeling basic assets, like walls, ceilings and doors, and use my trim texture to texture these. During this step I also discovered Material Functions in unreal which I used to create specific materials like iron or steel and blend these using masks in the actual material, I also made parameters to adjust the colors, roughness and dirt masks.

Once most of the blockout was replaced I wanted to get the factory feel in my level and created some props to spread around. After the level was build it was time for some lightning, I made use of blueprints to handle this and placed some lamps around the level.
